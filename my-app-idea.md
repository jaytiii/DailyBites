# DailyBite

## One-Sentence Summary

DailyBite is a social meal journal for food lovers who want to log their daily meals, share recipes with friends, and discover what others are eating — filtered by diet type, prep time, and ingredients.

## The Problem

Most people eat the same meals on repeat because they lack inspiration, and there's no easy way to share what you're actually cooking day-to-day with friends. Existing recipe apps are overwhelming and impersonal. DailyBite keeps it simple: log what you eat, share it with your circle, and discover real meals that real people are making right now.

## User Types

- **Registered users**: All users have the same capabilities — they can log meals, post recipes, browse the social feed, and view other users' profiles and meal journals.

## Core Features

- Log daily meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner, or snack) with a title and personal notes
- Create a recipe card for each meal with ingredients, steps, cook time, and diet label (Vegan / Vegetarian / Non-Veg)
- Browse a social feed of meals posted by all users
- Filter the feed by diet label, prep time (Quick / Medium / Slow), or search by ingredient
- View any user's profile to explore their full meal journal and recipes

## What Users See

### Screen 1: Social Feed
A scrollable feed of meals posted by all users, showing:
- Meal title and type (breakfast / lunch / dinner / snack)
- Diet label badge (🌱 Vegan, 🥦 Vegetarian, 🍗 Non-Veg)
- Prep time tag (⚡ Quick, 🕐 Medium, 🍲 Slow)
- Posted by (username, links to their profile)

At the top, users can filter by diet label, prep time, or type an ingredient to search.

### Screen 2: Log a Meal
A form to add a new meal entry with fields for:
- Meal type (breakfast / lunch / dinner / snack)
- Meal title (e.g. "Chickpea Stir Fry", "Avocado Toast")
- Date
- Notes (optional, e.g. "Added extra chilli today")
- Option to attach a recipe

### Screen 3: Recipe Detail Page
Shows a full recipe linked to a meal entry:
- Ingredients list
- Step-by-step instructions
- Total cook time
- Diet label and prep time tag

### Screen 4: My Journal
A personal weekly view of the logged-in user's meals:
- Calendar grid showing meals logged per day
- Click any day to see full meal entries
- Options to edit or delete your own entries

### Screen 5: User Profile
Shows another user's public meal activity:
- Display name and bio
- Grid of their past meal entries
- Clickable recipe cards for meals that have recipes attached

## Example Scenario

Priya is a college student trying to eat healthier but always runs out of lunch ideas. She signs up for DailyBite and logs her Monday lunch — a chickpea stir fry — tagging it Vegan and Quick (under 15 mins). She adds a recipe with 5 ingredients and simple steps.

Her friend Marco opens the social feed, filters by "Vegan" and "Quick," and finds Priya's post. He clicks through to her profile, browses her past week of meals, and decides to try her recipe for dinner. That evening, Marco logs his own version with a small twist — extra chilli and a squeeze of lemon — and posts it to the feed.

The next morning, Priya sees Marco's version on the feed and gets inspired to try his twist. Neither of them had to scroll through a recipe app or Google — they just followed what a real friend was actually cooking.
